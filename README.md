
<p>Our vision for BioRevive: New Horizons was to imagine an optimistic future for the Earth in 2100. Although humanity’s use of fossil fuels and destruction of habitats will wreak havoc on Earth’s ecosystems, humanity’s innovation can restore these ecosystems. </p>
<p>We wanted to show how a wide variety of sciences – bioengineering, agriculture, technology – could come together to restore the Earth’s ecosystems. We also wanted to show a range of technologies used in each ecosystem, especially the Australian coral reefs, Brazilian rainforests, and the Arctic. Also, in the future, feeding an estimated population of up to 11.2 billion people will be more difficult. We wanted to address this with the inclusion of a futuristic farm.</p>
<p>The design of the game allows the player to jump between different ecosystems and restore them.</p>
<h2>Gameplay</h2>
<a href = "https://drive.google.com/file/d/1aiSb_xOpehhaoqHUB5-iColdQuoPl5wQ/view?usp=drive_link">BioRevive Gameplay</a> involves a repetitive cycle with key differences across each ecosystem that is restored. As the restoration specialist, you arrive in an ecosystem fully destroyed. You are able to earn money by completing restorative tasks like releasing extinct species, freezing the arctic, and growing the Coral reefs. In each game, you work in a biotech lab, where you are able to create your own organisms by fully customizing the genome of lab-generated cells

<h2>Coral Reef</h2>
<img width="956" height="539" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bfaf757a-8baa-447f-aedc-5623a6437b9a" />

We were inspired by the use of Coral concrete in Indonesia and the Caribbean coastal reefs to bring back life to the reefs. They work by creating a structure that coral can attach and grow on. Then, the coral can become a home for smaller organisms and restore the ecosystem. 
A Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution study showed that playing sounds of healthy ocean reefs encouraged coral larvae to attach, and could be a crucial part of coral restoration. After researching current technologies, we decided to include both coral concrete and an Audio Amplifier in our Coral Reef Ecosystem Game to show how these advances in ocean science could help coral reefs.

<h2>Arctic</h2>
A major problem in the Arctic is the melting of the ice cap due to global warming and increased Greenhouse Gasses in the atmosphere. We decided to depict this with floating CO2 clouds in the Arctic level. Then, we learned about DAC (Direct Air Capture), a technology that can extract CO2 from the atmosphere and make products. Inspired by this tech, we decided to add a machine in the game that could take out CO2 from the atmosphere and earn the player money 

<h2>Rainforest</h2>
<img width="959" height="540" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8f738fb9-75e7-4479-b3be-c514f05c8493" />
In the future, the Brazilian Amazon Rainforest will experience increased droughts and dryness that will put it at an increased risk for destructive forest-fires. Currently, states like California and countries like Australia and Israel use AI-powered drones to spot fires. We envisioned that Ecosystem restoration will also incorporate these AI powered drones to reduce fires.

<h2>The Biotech Lab</h2>
<img width="959" height="449" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6f172c6c-25cb-42ab-9391-b1a27c1e5bc3" />
Since Rosalind Franklin discovered the shape of DNA in 1953, biologists have made enormous leaps in biotechnology. We have gained the power to change DNA with CRISPR in 1987 and access to the human genome in the 2000s. In the future, our advances in biotech will allow humans to resurrect long-extinct animals like polar bears and create incredible super-crops like the PluroCulture Crops. Our lab showcases the ability to create new organisms from scratch in the future.

<h2>Futuristic Farm</h2>
<img width="959" height="542" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2ed09851-ae2d-4bbd-be21-51c8937aa0ba" />
PolyCulture (Intercropping) involves growing many different crops together, and improves food yield and biodiversity. However, it isn’t commonly used in industries because labor costs are expensive and caring for each crop differently is difficult. Instead, many farms use Monocultures, which are cheap to grow, but reduce biodiversity and are susceptible to disease. In the future, we imagined that PluroCulture Crops could solve this problem: farms could grow many varieties of the same kind of plant (that need the same kinds of care). Since each crop’s genetic code would be made different, they would be more resistant to disease.
